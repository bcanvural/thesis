Position Summary:


The Ophthalmic Imaging Research Laboratory in the Department of Ophthalmology
seeks a Data Analyst. Responsibilities will include operation of ocular imaging
devices, image acquisition from human and animal subjects, data collection and
analysis. Obtaining quality images of human and animal models is required. The
individual must have at least an undergraduate degree in Bioengineering or
Physics. A background in biomedical engineering specializing in medical imaging
and lasers, experience in retinal functional, anatomical, and metabolic imaging
is advantageous. Must be able to work independently as well as with a group and
have excellent interpersonal skills.

Job Responsibilities:

  * The individual will be solely responsible for the operation of ocular
    imaging devices This responsibility also requires maintaining the devices
    in good operational status which could include troubleshooting, cleaning
    and calibration of the equipment. Will be solely responsible for exporting
    images and raw image data for further processing.
  * The individual is responsible for data collection.
  * The incumbent will be responsible for image analysis. The processing of the
    images will be captured using state of the art software. This individual
    should be able to work independently.
  * The incumbent will be responsible for image acquisition. Obtaining good
    quality images of human and animal models is a mandated responsibility for
    this individual. The incumbent will be responsible for acquiring quality
    images on a daily basis under minimal supervision under a strict deadline.

Minimum Qualifications:
To qualify you must have an undergraduate degree in Bioengineering or Physics.
At least 2 years experience or equivalent combination of education and
experience. The individual must be willing to work independently under a strict
timeline. This job will require data collection and analysis so incumbent must
proficient with regards to data collection and analysis since any deviation
could lead to miscalculations with data analysis. This individual will be
working directly with physicians, students, residents and other staff as well
as contact with potential research subjects. Good inter-personal skills and the
ability to work as part of a group is essential. Must be able to lift 20 pounds
or less. The incumbent must be able to assis potential research subjects to a
sitting position in order to capture the necessary images. Must be able to lift
20 pounds or less. A background in biomedical engineering specializing in
medical imaging and lasers, experience in retinal functional, anatomical, and
metabolic imaging is advantageous.

Qualified candidates must be able to effectively communicate with all levels of
the organization.

Data Scientist

  * United States
  * Full Time
  * Product Management
  * Mid Level

  *  
  *  
  *  

Rover.com is seeking a Data Scientist to lead the design of our next generation
user experience testing and machine learning infrastructure. The Rover.com user
experience includes desktop and mobile web as well as mobile app; the
infrastructure for which the Data Scientist is responsible ranges from simple A
/B testing to multi-armed bandit approaches to optimizing long-running
experiments with significant impact on how users interact with Rover.com,
participate in our marketplace, and find care for their dogs.

The Data Scientist will act as an expert source of best practices in all
aspects of experimental design. The ideal candidate will have demonstrated
experience in online user testing, and will be well-versed and able to speak to
the unique challenges of optimizing user experience in an online/mobile
environment, including statistical power requirements, the pros and cons of
various approaches to defining control groups, how to isolate the impact of
user experience changes from other factors such as geographical effects, and so
on.

The Data Scientist will also act as a domain expert across all aspects of
machine learning. The ideal candidate will be able to lead the design of our
next-generation machine learning infrastructure, and will have demonstrated
experience working with such systems in a real-world, online environment.

The person in this role must be comfortable with imperfect data and understand
bias but always strive for actionable and clear results. Outstanding
communication skills, including the ability to translate learning into clear
recommendations to the business, are essential.

Experience working in a marketplace environment is strongly preferred.

The Data Scientist will be proficient with statistical tools such as Saas and
R, and well-versed in SQL. At least some experience with other programming
languages (e.g., C++, Java, Python, etc) and big-data tools is
preferred. Qualified candidates will have a background in statistics, math,
applied math, or a similarly quantitative discipline. An advanced degree is
preferred.

 

About Rover

Rover.com connects dog owners with a nationwide network of dog lovers for hire.
Rover makes it easy for dog owners throughout the U.S. to reach sitters who
offer in-home boarding, pet sitting, dog walking, and day care. More than 92
percent of the population lives within a short drive of one of Rover?s 25,000
sitters.

We are a rapidly growing and well-financed startup founded and based in
downtown Seattle.

Our style

  * Data-informed decision making with rigorous split testing.
  * Integrated teams with developers, designers and analysts working
    side-by-side.
  * Dogs in the office. Bring yours, too!

Our tools

  * SQL, Postgres, Redshift
  * Excel & Google Sheets
  * Google Analytics
  * R & Python

Why we?ll hire you

  * You?re a deeply analytical thinker who loves using analysis to make
    decisions
  * You can take abstractly defined problems and work with (imperfect) data to
    drive decisions
  * You are your own QA team, producing quality analysis and spot checking or
    comparing to known sources to ensure accuracy
  * You can pitch your own ideas and shepherd them through to completion
  * You show pattern of being self-started, self-taught and self-directed
  * You favor open communication with willingness to ask and answer questions
  * You are output- and outcome-oriented
  * You have excellent written and verbal communication skills
  * You play nicely with others (and dogs)

Experience and Education

  * B.A or B.S required; advanced degree preferred
  * Strong knowledge of statistical analysis
  * Experience in online user testing
  * Knowledge of SQL

This position is based at Rover HQ in Downtown Seattle.  Competitive salary &
benefits depending on experience.  Candidates must be eligible to work in the
US.

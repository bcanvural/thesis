Senior Systems Support Analyst

Apply Company The Boeing Company Job ID 1700000026 Date posted 01/03/2017
Location Renton Washington United States
Job Description
This position provides an opportunity to work in the fast paced world of
delivering 737 airplanes while helping to navigate the airplane program through
interesting and challenging regulatory compliance issues. As a technical leader
in the Renton Delivery Certification team you will work with Boeing design and
certification organizations to ensure all activities required for certification
of Boeing commercial airplanes are accomplished. This requires working with BCA
Engineering, Airplane Level Integration Team, and the RA teams while partnering
with FAA's Boeing Aviation Safety Oversight Office (BASOO) to ensure new
airplane projects are certified and ready for delivery on schedule. Successful
candidates must apply an interdisciplinary, collaborative approach to lead
planning, developing and verifying complex certification or validations
solutions.

Primary responsibilities include:
• Provide expert industry insight by participating in industry/regulator
activities to develop proposals for aviation regulatory requirements that
affect the design, production and operation of aircraft.
• Develop and document certification related processes, tools and procedures to
continuously improve the performance of certification tasks.
• Facilitate seamless integration of Post-Amendment Type certification activity
into 737 MAX into Production System.
• Manage the development of airplane flight testing Type Inspection
Authorizations (TIA) certification approvals prior to flight test.
• Manage aircraft certification and Organization Delegation Authority projects
for major changes to type design for Federal Aviation Administration, European
Aviation Safety Association, and Transport Canada Civil Aviation projects.
• Address Certification Plans and compliance Deliverables.
• Understand, explain, and apply Boeing Organization Designation Authorization
(ODA) Procedures Manual and affected processes and procedures.
• Work with the Federal Aviation Administration, particularly the Boeing
Aviation Safety Oversight Office (BASOO) and interface with Project
Administrators and Authorized Representatives.
• Participate in preliminary design activities to provide advice and counsel on
pertinent regulatory requirements, reviewing preliminary configurations, and
making suggestions to enhance the certification of the new products such that
it minimized certification risks.
• Define the regulatory certification requirements for a specific product
including resolution of issues, with the regulators.
• Monitor potential new regulations and communicates impact throughout the
Enterprise as appropriate.
• Develop and deliver training in the use of new processes, tools, and
procedures.
• Oversee the development of multiple certification plans, including providing
technical ownership as appropriate, and negotiate changes required in order to
secure approval of certification plan by regulatory agencies.
• Provide oversight including reviewing the certification plan to assure it is
complete, complies with certification basis and is coordinated with suppliers
and partners.
• Review of certification data for accuracy, facilitate necessary changes,
communicate pertinent changes, track program to certification schedule,
document data corrections, assist program owners in resolving certification
issues, and transmitting certification data to regulators.
• Integrate airplane certification activities within the Boeing 737 Renton
Delivery Certification Group ensuring all requirements that support the
achievement of Aircraft Certification Eligibility, are accomplished prior to
customer delivery.
• Work closely with Systems, Propulsion, Structures, Interiors and Flight Test
engineering teams to manage the certification of airplane changes that support
a fast paced delivery schedule of 42 airplanes per month (increasing to 47/mo
in 2017).
• Communicate to Senior Management the status of certification schedules and
issues/risks to ensure airplane delivery schedules are met.
• Using the certification plan as a basis, develop a compliance checklist to
track, document and report to the program owners and regulatory agencies that
certification basis requirements are met.

Qualifications
Required Qualifications:

  • This position must meet Export Control compliance requirements, therefore a
    “US Person” as defined by 22 C.F.R. § 120.15 is required. “US Person”
    includes US Citizen, lawful permanent resident, refugee, or asylee.
  • At least 5 years of experience leading an engineering project or team.
  • At least 5 years of experience working with Foreign Authority Regulations?
    (Ex: European Aviation Safety Association, Transport Canada Civil Aviation,
    or similar).
  • At least 10 years of experience working with Federal Aviation
    Administration (FAA) Aircraft Certification Offices.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Experience working with certification procedures for the Federal Aviation
    Administration (FAA) Military Certification Office.
  • Experience conducting/leading QMS Audits.

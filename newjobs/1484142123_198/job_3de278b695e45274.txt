Energy Data Scientist

300 Energy Services ? Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania
Apply for this Job
Department 300 Energy Services
Employment Type Full-Time
Minimum Experience Experienced
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

kWantera is looking for an Energy Data Scientist to extend our quant energy
trading platform. Our ideal candidate will have extensive hands-on experience
in developing mathematical algorithms and a broad knowledge of statistics,
machine learning, optimization, and financial math. Strong communication skills
 and commercial decision-making abilities will round out the ideal candidate's
profile.

The Data Scientist will be an integral part of our energy analytics team,
therefore, responsible for: 

? Developing and testing electricity and natural gas price forecasting
algorithms using large datasets such as load, weather, historical, grid,
forward markets etc.

 ? Developing and testing arbitrage algorithms using our proprietary energy
market models, our price forecasts, and customers' energy portfolio.

  * Close collaboration with our Sales team as well as direct customer contact
    when needed to establish performance goals and communicate trending against
    goals
  * Verbalizing decision-making process to internal and external stakeholders
  * Identifying and implementing commercially scalable processes

 ? Working in collaboration with the software engineering team in deploying the
developed models tailored to specific customer needs.

 ? Participating in the software development process, testing, and debugging
required to support the deployed models.

  > Strong programming background in Python, with experience in numpy, scipy, 
scikit-learn and pandas preferred. 

> Knowledge of large-scale software development including strong background in
OOP, and knowledge of Git, SVN or other version control software.

> Experience working with large databases to access, manipulate and process
data. Knowledge of MongoDB or other nosql database implementations.

> MS degree or PhD (preferred) in Mathematics, Physics, Engineering, Computer
Science or other quantitative discipline.

> Minimum of 2-5 years of work experience in quantitative modeling. Academic
work can be accepted in lieu of job experience on a case-by-case basis.

> Candidate must be a team player with strong sense of accountability and
desire to continuously learn

 

 

Our ideal candidate will be excited to work in the energy commodity industry 
(i.e., buy, sell, and manage). We offer competitive compensation commensurate
with experience, education, and references.

All your information will be kept confidential according to EEO guidelines.

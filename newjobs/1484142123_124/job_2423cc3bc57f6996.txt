Description:

The Intelligence & Homeland Security Group has a current opening for a Data
Scientist


The candidate will conduct research to advanced Motion GEOINT Tradecraft and
Technology, propose methods for automating existing and new processes to more
effectively utilize content extracted from Motion GEOINT data sources, develop
approaches for measuring and improving the quality and confidence of
information generated by automated processes. They shall provide support at the
system-level with the development of algorithms, data processing and signal
processing. The candidate will provide supports with the modeling of the
overall system, including design of the model architecture and interfaces,
analytical and empirical models, simulations, analysis and validation.
Additionally they shall provide support for the implementation and testing of
algorithms and designs. They could possibly evaluate the quality of algorithms
by using statistical methods and provides recommendations to the development
team.
Utilizes multi-disciplined expertise in either/combined statistical,
mathematical, predictive modeling, geospatial science skills to orally and
visually provide explanations of mass data sets. Utilizes latest tools,
techniques and methodologies, to interpret and communicate findings in creative
ways beyond prevailing perspectives. Uses programming skills to develop
necessary algorithms capable of analyzing massive data sets. Programming
language examples include but are not limited to SAS, R, Python, Java, Ruby,
Clojure, Matlab, C++, et al.


Qualifications:

Minimum of 15 years of relevant work experience with Bachelor's Degree or
Master's Degree with 13 yrs of experience

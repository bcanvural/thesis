Requisition ID:  25106 

Job Function/Category:  Finance / Accounting 

Employment Type:  Exempt Full Time 

This is an exciting time to be joining PSEG. Our commitments, which include
safety, integrity, customer focus, and diversity & inclusion, are the fabric of
our culture and help drive the success of our business. We are fortunate to
have an outstanding workforce of diverse and highly skilled talent who move us
forward in our operational excellence journey.  PSEG has more than 12,000
employees who are dedicated to the communities we serve and embody our vision:
People providing Safe, Reliable, Economic and Greener Energy

NOTE: This is a multi-level position. Candidate will be placed at the
appropriate level depending upon skills and abilities.

The successful candidate will work closely with marketers, traders, front
office quantitative analysts, portfolio directors, and risk directors
throughout the transaction process and will be responsible for facilitating the
accurate valuation and independent risk oversight of energy structured
transactions and various other energy derivatives.
The analyst will be part of a team responsible for independently developing and
validating energy derivative valuation models including but not limited to:
wholesale energy full-requirements (and other load following) contracts, retail
energy contracts, heat rate options, power plant tolling agreements, natural
gas storage contracts, natural gas transportation contracts, and interest rate
derivatives, and other non-standard commodity structured transactions.
Work as part of team to ensure all applicable structured / non-standard
transactions are captured and accurately modeled in the Enterprise Risk
Management department’s energy risk system.
Development and maintenance of option implied volatility surfaces which may be
required for derivative valuations. Accurately update period-end valuations and
perform hedge effectiveness assessments as applicable. Ensure compliance with
valuation controls and associated reporting requirements for derivative
instruments.
Maintain procedures and documentation governing the methodologies and processes
followed by team. Continuously examine relevant financial and energy risk
literature to ensure that the most appropriate models and the most accurate
statistical techniques are being employed.

Required Skills, Competencies, Education or Experience:

 

  • Bachelor’s degree in Economics, Quantitative/Computational Finance,
    Mathematics, Computer Science, or another related quantitative field.
  • Two years of prior applicable experience or graduate degree in a related
    discipline.
  • Ability to perform valuation of financial & physical derivatives including
    forwards, futures, swaps, options, spread options, and other exotic
    options.
  • Proficient in Microsoft Excel.
  • Knowledge of R, Matlab, S-PLUS or another statistical programming package.
  • Familiarity with the power / natural gas / energy trading.
  • Detail oriented with strong analytical skills.
  • Excellent organizational skills with ability to simultaneously handle
    multiple duties.
     


Desired Skills, Competencies, Education or Experience (Sr. Analyst)

 

  • Master’s degree in quantitative discipline (or MBA).
  • Minimum of three years prior related applicable experience in quantitative
    finance, financial risk management, or energy related structuring
    (commercial) role.
  • Valuation experience with energy full-requirements transactions, power and
    gas retail contracts, power plants, tolling agreements.
  • Knowledge of risk management fundamentals, derivative pricing concepts, and
    option theory.
  • Good decision making and problem solving ability.

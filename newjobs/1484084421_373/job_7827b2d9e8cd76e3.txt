Date: Dec 14, 2016

Location: Gwynedd, PA, US

Company: MSD

2017 Data Science and Predictive Modeling Intern:ADM008154

Description

Merck & Co., Inc. Kenilworth, N.J., U.S.A. known as Merck in the United States
and Canada, is a global health care leader with a diversified portfolio of
prescription medicines, vaccines and animal health products. Today, we are
building a new kind of healthcare company ? one that is ready to help create a
healthier future for all of us.

Our ability to excel depends on the integrity, knowledge, imagination, skill,
diversity and teamwork of an individual like you. To this end, we strive to
create an environment of mutual respect, encouragement and teamwork. As part of
our global team, you?ll have the opportunity to collaborate with talented and
dedicated colleagues while developing and expanding your career.


Merck Research Labs (MRL) is currently recruiting for a Data Science and
Predictive Modeling intern for the summer of 2017.

The Intern will develop data and analytic visualization methods to generate
insights for communicating the value of healthcare technologies aimed at
improving population health. Projects may be product-specific or focused on
issues spanning multiple products.

At the conclusion of the term, the Intern will share results and recommended
actions as a final project presentation.

This is a paid internship targeted to start in May/June 2017 for 10-12 weeks.

Applicants should have an interest in supporting human health and drug
discovery, and have the ability to compare and contrast results from various
methodologies, and recommend the best techniques. If you are the kind of
individual who thrives on challenge and possesses the technical, leadership and
business skills that are of value to our business, we invite you to apply.

A letter of reference from a professor is highly recommended; you may attach it
in the same section of the profile as your resume.

Qualifications

Education:

- Required: Must be currently pursuing a Masters or PhD degree in Statistics,
Data Science, Applied Mathematics, Computer Science, business, economics,
informatics, policy, or related quantitative field.

- Strong candidates in an undergraduate program will also be considered.

Required Experience:

- Must have completed a minimum of (2) semesters of graduate study or (4)
semesters of undergraduate study by May 31, 2017.

- Candidates must be returning to school in Fall 2017.

- Minimum of (1) year of healthcare domain knowledge in life sciences, health
data, health IT and/or the payor marketplace.

- Experience in applying real-world healthcare data in a health-related
industry, academic or research setting.

- An understanding of data mining algorithms and experience applying advanced
techniques to large data sets

- Persistent and driven

- Demonstrated characteristics of a forward thinker and self-motivator that
thrives on new challenges and adapts to learning new knowledge

- Entrepreneurial and intellectual curiosity in the application and
communication of health data.

Preferred Experience:

- Knowledge in advanced analytics and machine learning techniques. This
includes expertise in analytics platforms such as Matlab, R, Python; research
experience in analyzing big data sets

- Experience working with longitudinal real world data assets (e.g., claims,
electronic medical records, genomic data, consumer data, social media data,
wearables)

- Understanding of business processes in one or more of discovery, research,
clinical, commercial, regulatory or safety domains

- An understanding of the payment reform or payer-provider structure.

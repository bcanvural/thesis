New York Life Insurance Company (?New York Life? or ?the company?) is the
largest mutual life insurance company in the United States*. Founded in 1845,
New York Life is headquartered in New York City, maintains offices in all fifty
states, and owns Seguros Monterrey New York Life in Mexico.

 

New York Life is one of the most financially strong and highly capitalized
insurers in the business. The company reported 2015 operating earnings of
$1.875 billion. Total assets under management at year end 2015, with
affiliates, totaled $528 billion. New York Life holds the highest possible
financial strength ratings currently awarded to any life insurer from all four
of the major ratings agencies: A.M. Best, A++; Fitch AAA; Moody?s Aaa; Standard
& Poor?s AA+. (Source: Individual Third Party Ratings Report as of 8/11/15).

 

Financial strength, integrity and humanity?the values upon which New York Life
was founded?have guided the company?s decisions and actions for over 170 years.

 

We are looking to hire a Data Scientist / Statistician to tackle relevant
business concerns at New York Life.  The Analytic Science Division serves as an
internal consulting group for the company.  We are involved with an amazing
range of awesomely interesting projects that includes Marketing Analytics,
Customer Segmentation, Market Opportunity Analyses, predictive modeling work
and Fraud Risk prevention through unsupervised learning techniques.  We also
partner with actuaries and underwriters to lend our expertise on new and novel
approaches to age old problems and methodologies.

 

 A standard project would go like this:
 

Meet with business area to understand what the problem is that is being
addressed or what the current need is

Start mapping out a solution

Gather the data and use an appropriate methodology develop analytical solution
or model

Discuss with business area

Pilot, test, implement, maintain as necessary

It's really rewarding to be so involved in every project and to see your work
thoroughly appreciated and implemented.  You have independence in your work,
but the atmosphere here is very collaborative.  You'll have a lot of friends in
need.

 Some projects are smaller in scope.  Other responsibilities include
participating in discussions, representing the team at meetings and so on.

 

If this describes you, then you'll be perfect for the position:

 

  * You possess sound statistical knowledge in standard techniques:  Logistic
    Regression, Cluster Analysis, Random Forests, Experimental Design, etc.
  * You have an MS or PhD in quantitative field such as Statistics, Applied
    Mathematics, Physics, Computer Science or other field which is highly
    analytical / quantitative
  * You have some work experience with real data either through your current
    job or through projects during grad school
  * Your toolkit includes R, Python, SAS or Matlab
  * You have familiarity with SQL or can easily pick it up
  * You have some experience with a visualization tool such as Tableau or
    Spotfire or would like to learn because you love to learn new tools and
    like the idea of being able to create powerful visuals for our internal
    clients to go along with your intense modeling results
  * Of course, you can use Microsoft Office - Excel / Powerpoint / Word and
    have the attitude of Whatever I don't know I can learn
  * You love the surprise of discovery through data
  * You love making people understand how to solve a problems through an
    analytical perspective

  * You are so glad you chose being a data scientist for your career because if
    you have to work, this is a really cool way to spend your time

Compensation is based on education and experience.

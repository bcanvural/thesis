Overview

Cures Start Here. At Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center, home to three
Nobel laureates, interdisciplinary teams of world-renowned scientists seek new
and innovative ways to prevent, diagnose and treat cancer, HIV/AIDS and other
life-threatening diseases. Fred Hutch’s pioneering work in bone marrow
transplantation led to the development of immunotherapy, which harnesses the
power of the immune system to treat cancer with minimal side effects. An
independent, nonprofit research institute based in Seattle, Fred Hutch houses
the nation’s first and largest cancer prevention research program, as well as
the clinical coordinating center of the Women’s Health Initiative and the
international headquarters of the HIV Vaccine Trials Network. Careers Start
Here.

 

Performs data collection from medical records for patients treated with stem
cell transplantation. Responsible for the collection, coordination, processing,
and quality control of clinical data. Performs records reviews to abstract and
record highly specialized transplant-related medical data. Enters and verifies
data in a computerized database. These data are used for research studies about
the health of long-term survivors. The ideal candidate is very detail-oriented
and enjoys independent work while being part of a team.

The incumbent works closely with other Data Coordinators and under the
supervision of the Project Manager in performing work assignments. Refers
non-routine decisions to supervisor or clinical staff.

Responsibilities

  • Reviews and abstracts complex data from medical records, databases and data
    collection forms.
  • Abstracts and codes information such as patient treatment and diagnoses.
  • Performs routine audits to insure quality and completeness of the data
    submitted.
  • Serves as a liaison and reference resource to Physicians, Investigators,
    other staff members, and other organizations with questions about data
    collection or protocol procedures and requirements.
  • Updates procedures as changes are made.
  • Contacts physician offices for additional information.
  • Maintains computer database.
  • Works closely with Database Manager to ensure data quality.
  • Performs other duties as assigned.

Qualifications

  • High School graduation or the equivalent.
  • Minimum of two years of college.
  • One-two years of experience in medical records, cancer registry, or related
    field reviewing and collecting data from medical records.
  • Experience may substitute for education.
  • Strong computer skills and experience working with data bases.
  • Strong verbal and written communication skills.
  • Knowledge of medical terminology and research data management preferred.
    RRA or ART helpful.
  • Please attach a cover letter to your application detailing your prior
    relevant experience and specific interest in this position.

Research Scientist

Research Boston, Massachusetts

Under the supervision of Senior Scientist, Research Scientist is responsible
for the following duties:

  • Design and implement automated procedures to acquire various weather
    datasets (e.g. METAR, SYNOP) for the European Extratropical Cyclone (ETC)
    Model.
  • Administrate research SQL server database. Test and validate backups,
    performance analysis and tuning, security and integrity checks.
  • Implement and update current financial loss calculation module for
    China-MPCI model and US-crop-hail model with C# programming.
  • Create and update UNICEDE exposure database for the China-MPCI model and
    US-crop-hail model. From understand the standards to transfer existing file
    based exposure set into SQL database.
  • Proactively contribute to code performance optimizations and continuous
    improvements to code quality, robustness, and model development processes.
  • Search, collect and analyze data related to assessing agricultural risk,
    such as temperature, precipitation, crop yields, crop damage, loss data,
    etc.
  • Maintain and update the US county level crop yield data interpolation tool
    with R programming.
  • Collect all the relevant data and build crop yield and price databases for
    the US agriculture insurance market.
  • Execute the fund designation service for the current AIR clients for
    multitple sales closing dates through the year.
  • Write new computer codes to update and enhance the AIR Fund Designation
    Model.
  • Research the state of the art data mining methods, find the relevant
    applications and implement them into the AIR Fund Designation Model.
    Develop and test new data mining algorithms to enhance the fund designation
    optimization.   
  • Communicate the fund designation results with the AIR clients and explain
    the AIR modeling methodology in client meetings.

 

Minimum requirements include: Master’s degree in Computer Engineering, Computer
Science, or related field (or foreign degree equivalent) plus the following
special skills:

 

  • Demonstrated ability to gather, understand, and critically analyze large
    amounts of data;
  • Strong knowledge and practical experience of object/component-oriented
    design and programming language including C++, C#, and FORTRAN;
  • Experience of working with statistical packages R or Matlab;
  • Familiarity with manipulating large data sets using SQL for the risk
    assessment purposes;
  • Experience with Python or powershell scripting.

 

 

Location: 131 Dartmouth St, Boston, MA 02116

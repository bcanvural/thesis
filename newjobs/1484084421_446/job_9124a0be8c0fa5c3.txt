Associate Statistical Scientist/Statistical Scientist - Biomarker Focus

South San Francisco, California
Job ID: 00446883
Return to Search Results
 Apply For This Job  Download PDF

The Position

The Position:Genentech seeks statisticians with training and interest in
genomics or related fields for positions supporting and leading drug
development activities, including collaboration with biomarker scientists.
Teamwork, effective communication and leadership skills are essential.
Candidates should be experienced in data analysis and related statistical
methodologies, be passionate about personalized health care drug development
and expect to pursue projects involving statistical applications to molecular
data for clinical development.   Responsibilities:

  • Successfully represent biostatistics/other biometrics functions on
    development teams
  • Provide statistical and strategic leadership for clinical development
    projects,
  • Directly responsible for the statistical integrity, adequacy, and accuracy
    of the clinical studies within the project, including activities to support
    personalized healthcare strategy
  • Provides analytical and strategic leadership for exploratory activities
    such as personalized healthcare biomarker development and PK/PD modelling.
  • Company Biostatistics lead for Health Authority interactions
  • Collaborates and consults with biomarker scientists in support of clinical
    development projects and presentations/publications
  • Keep abreast of new developments in statistics, drug development, and
    regulatory guidance through literature review, conference attendance, etc.
  • Leads Biostatistics and cross-functional initiatives
  • Leads the development of functional processes
  • Contributes to the biostatistics/scientific community by mentoring junior
    colleagues, actively serving as subject matter expert and participating in
    internal/external initiatives

Who You Are

Qualifications:

  • MSc or PhD in statistics/biostatistics or a related field, with relevant
    experience
  • Sound knowledge of theoretical and applied statistics
  • Experience with statistical software such as R, S-Plus, SAS
  • Basic understanding of molecular biology, cancer, immunology, genetics or
    genomics in addition to standard molecular assays and technologies (e.g.
    qRT-PCR, immunohistochemistry, modern sequencing approaches) will be a
    strong plus
  • Effective communication and collaboration skills, including good
    statistical consulting and problem-solving skills
  • Demonstrated leadership potential
  • Prior experience in clinical drug development is preferred

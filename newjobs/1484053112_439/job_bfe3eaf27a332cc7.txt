Job Description

We are looking for a passionate, data obsessed person to join our Business
Intelligence (BI) team, a group responsible for building and scaling the data
science function throughout Pilot Flying J. As a data engineer, you will own
the building and lifecycle, of fast, fault-tolerant infrastructure that
converts unorganized, messy data streams into reliable, highly-available data
sources.

This position will require someone with:

* Bachelor’s degree or higher in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, or
related field
* Professional software engineering experience: 4 years (BSc), 2 years (MSc), 1
year (PhD)
* Mastery and comprehensive expertise in one of Java or Scala
* Production level familiarity with the Hadoop ecosystem
* Familiarity with building streaming data pipelines using Kafka + Storm/Samza/
Spark Streaming
* Intimately familiar with cloud computing on AWS or Azure
* Proficient in a scripting language (Python, Unix shell, Perl, etc.)
* Experience with relational databases, Mapreduce systems, and MPP platforms
* Strong CS fundamentals and ENG practices and a desire to share them with
others
* Able to communicate effectively - ready to give us a demo of your favorite
project?
* Able to set priorities, work independently and follow up problems to
resolution
* Love collaborative work and like being part of a team that has goals,
deadlines, and owns its work
* Have a passion for simplifying the world while striving to keep every byte of
data

The basic duties of this position include:

* Work with data scientists to build scalable pipelines, model deployments, and
visualization platform
* Lead the development of data architecture and data warehousing modernization
efforts
* Develop and maintain monitored, simple ETL jobs focused on data quality
* Optimize pipelines and data sources based on usage patterns
* Automate builds, tests, and deployments for low-touch improvements and
maintenance
* Implement systems for tracking data quality and consistency
* Experimenting with new technologies (e.g. Druid, VoltDB, etc.) to help
systems evolve and mature
